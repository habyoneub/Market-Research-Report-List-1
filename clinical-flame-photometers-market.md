<p><p>Clinical flame photometers are analytical instruments used to measure the concentration of certain metal ions, such as sodium, potassium, lithium, and calcium, in biological fluids. These devices work on the principle of flame emission spectroscopy, where a sample is introduced into a flame, causing the ions to emit light at characteristic wavelengths. The intensity of this light correlates to the concentration of the specific ion, enabling healthcare professionals to monitor various conditions, such as electrolyte imbalances and kidney function.</p><p>The Clinical Flame Photometers Market is experiencing significant growth driven by the rising prevalence of chronic diseases, along with an increasing focus on early diagnosis and preventive healthcare.
