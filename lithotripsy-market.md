<p><p>The lithotripsy market comprises three main types: extracorporeal lithotripsy (ESWL), which uses shock waves from outside the body to break stones; intracorporeal lithotripsy, where devices are inserted to fragment stones directly within the body; and percutaneous lithotripsy, which involves a small incision to access and treat stones via specialized instruments. Each method varies in technique, invasiveness, and suitability, catering to different patient needs and types of urinary or biliary stones, thereby driving diverse market opportunities.</p></p>
